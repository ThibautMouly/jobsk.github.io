Why Bratislava?

Economy

A Growing Environment

Major multinational companies are settled down in Bratislava because of its strong government support, strategic location and rich cultural environment.

Slovakia is now one of the most attractive investment locations in Central Europe. Many internationally reputed companies have either started establishing or are planning to establish their service and outsourcing centers in Bratislava.

All these companies are searching for multilingual candidates to improve and develop their business here.

European Union

Slovakia is part of the European Union since 2004.

The common European currency was adopted on 1st of January 2009. ​The Slovak Republic is a parliamentary democracy with autonomous legislative, executive and judicial branches. The country is also part of the Schengen area which is a territory of 25 European countries. In this territory people can cross the border without passport control.

Social Life

The Capital City

Bratislava is the political, economic and cultural centre of Slovakia. It is home to several universities, museums, theatres, galleries and other important cultural and educational institutions. Bratislava is the biggest city of Slovakia with approximately 450,000 people.

Many young students and international people are giving the city vibrancy and dynamism. Cafes, bars and clubs in the center are combining historical ambience of the Old Town with youthful energy.

Bratislava is a great place where to be!

The Center of Europe

Bratislava is located in the extreme west of Slovakia, very close to Vienna (Austria).

Prague (Czech Republic) and Budapest (Hungary) are easy to reach by car or train.
